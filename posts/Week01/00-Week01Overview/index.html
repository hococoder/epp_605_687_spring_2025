Welcome to Week 1! This week, we'll dive right into working with Swift to build the foundations of an app that we'll work on all semester long. If you haven't gone through the material from "Week 0", please do so before proceeding; that will ensure your software is setup correctly, which is important when following along with the material in this course as well as completing and handing in assignments. Here's an overview of what we'll cover this week:

  • Introduction to Swift
    • We'll go over the basics of Swift, show how it is similar to other languages like Java, and most importantly highlight what makes Swift different from the other languages out there - and how programmers can take advantage of those differences when developing their apps.
  • Making a Gradebook App
    • Throughout the semester, we will work on parts of a Gradebook app together. Designed for Students, it will allow them to view listings of classes they can register for, class members, get information on the teacher, read assignment descriptions, and even upload files for submission (note: we won't use this feature to submit homeworks... it's just an example app!)
  • Building the Model
    • All good apps have a data model behind it - we'll define the structs, classes and other Swift types that allow us to define the model for the app
  • Programming Assignment 1
    • Your first assignment will center around making a model for a ToDo app, to get us ready to work on the user interface next week
  • Development Log 1
    • This week you'll also work on your first Development Log - where you'll define the requirements for the app you'll design and make as your final project for the class.
